# Bone White Theme Guidelines

## Theme name

**Bone White**  
_Calm, spacious, reflective; mortality as clarity._

---

## Core concept

Bone White is not about death as spectacle, gloom, or fear. It is about **memento mori as a clarifier**: the awareness that life is finite, and therefore this moment matters.

The page should feel like a **quiet daily rite**. It should not read as a productivity dashboard, a lifestyle app, or a dramatic meditation on death. It should feel like **morning light on paper**, with the composure of something **inscribed, kept, and returned to**.

The emotional effect is not urgency or melancholy. It is:

**stillness with consequence.**

---

## Core intention

A gentle daily nudge toward presence.  
A respectful encounter with finitude that makes the user feel:

- more awake
- more honest
- more grounded in the present day

This page is not warning the user. It is helping them **meet the day sincerely**.

---

## Design thesis

**Mortality is expressed through restraint.**  
The theme should communicate finitude not through dark imagery or heavy symbolism, but through:

- spaciousness
- disciplined hierarchy
- warm materiality
- quiet seriousness
- a single sincere interaction

Bone White should feel like a meeting point of:

- paper
- candle wax
- limestone
- old margins
- faint inscription

Fragile and enduring at once.

---

## Emotional frame

The theme should hold three tensions at the same time:

### 1. Fragility and permanence

Warm paper-like tones and faint texture suggest the temporary, the human, the perishable.  
Centered composition, inscribed typography, and visual restraint suggest memory, continuity, and what remains.

### 2. Silence and gravity

Whitespace creates silence.  
Typography and structure give that silence weight.

### 3. Mortality and invitation

The page should never say “be afraid.”  
It should say:

**because life is finite, this day deserves care.**

---

## Design principles

### 1. Treat the page as a ritual object

The screen should feel less like an interface and more like a small threshold the user crosses each day.

### 2. Use only what matters

Memento mori is about essentials. Every visual and textual element should justify its existence.

### 3. Let warmth soften the weight

Nothing should feel cold, sterile, metallic, or severe. Bone White should remain humane.

### 4. Avoid spectacle

No overt morbidity. No dramatic skull iconography. No gothic heaviness. No theatrical darkness.

### 5. Aim for presence, not performance

The page should not motivate in a self-help voice. It should quietly clarify.

---

## Visual direction

### Mood keywords

**Still • warm • contemplative • human • inscribed • finite • morning light on paper**

### Visual character

The interface should feel:

- calm, but not blank
- minimal, but not trendy
- elegant, but not luxurious for its own sake
- symbolic, but never illustrative
- emotionally grounded, not decorative

---

## Color palette

### Primary palette

- **Background:** warm bone / off-white  
  Feels like paper, wax, plaster, or sunlit stone. Never sterile white.

- **Primary text:** near-black charcoal  
  Softer than pure black; serious without harshness.

- **Secondary text:** soft graphite gray  
  Quiet and readable; never so faint that it feels evasive.

- **Lines / dividers:** very faint gray  
  Thin, restrained, almost like a pencil mark or page edge.

### Accent palette

Use **one accent only**, and use it sparingly.

Recommended options:

- **Muted dried rose** for tenderness and mortality
- **Muted gold** for sacredness and warmth

The accent should appear rarely, such as:

- focus outline
- a tiny emphasis detail
- a subtle active state

It should never dominate the page.

---

## Typography

### Conceptual roles

- **Serif:** memory, inscription, endurance, reflection
- **Sans-serif:** clarity, usability, the living present

### Usage

- **Headline / central phrase:** elegant serif with strong readability  
  It should feel carved or printed with intention, not ornamental.

- **Body copy / labels / inputs / UI text:** clean sans-serif  
  Quiet, simple, unobtrusive.

### Tone of typography

- slightly increased letter spacing on the title
- light to regular weights overall
- minimal use of bold
- emphasis should come from spacing, scale, and placement more than weight

### Desired feeling

The headline should feel **inscribed**, not shouted.

---

## Texture and surface

Texture should be present only as atmosphere.

### Recommended treatment

- a very faint paper grain or noise layer
- low opacity only
- no obvious distressed effects
- nothing that makes the page look vintage for its own sake

The texture should feel like something noticed only indirectly, the way paper reveals itself in light.

---

## Symbolic watermark

Use **one symbolic form only**.

Recommended options:

- a thin circle
- a minimal hourglass
- a faint clock face without numbers
- a single candle outline

### Best fit for this theme

A **minimal hourglass** is the strongest symbolic choice because it most directly conveys passage and finitude, but it must be abstract enough to feel atmospheric rather than illustrative.

### Watermark rules

- large and centered behind the main content
- opacity around 2–5%
- barely perceived
- never treated like an icon or decoration

It should feel half-seen, like memory.

---

## Layout concept: Welcome page

### Overall role

This page is a **daily threshold**.  
Its structure should feel ceremonial rather than merely functional.

### Layout sequence

The page should follow this emotional rhythm:

1. **Witness** — acknowledge the day
2. **Reflection** — receive the reminder
3. **Intention** — name one sincere thing
4. **Crossing** — move into the day

### Composition

- single-screen centered layout
- generous margins
- restrained width
- strong vertical rhythm
- lots of breathing room

The page should feel like it has already been quiet for a long time before the user arrives.

---

## Welcome page structure

### Quiet header

A small line near the top that situates the user in the day.

Examples:

- “Sunday, January 25”
- “Today”
- “A gentle reminder.”
- “This day will not return.”

It should feel like a whisper, not a title.

### Central anchor

The centerpiece is:

**MEMENTO MORI**

It should be:

- large, but not loud
- serif
- charcoal
- letter-spaced
- centered
- visually stable

### Supporting line

A single line beneath the title that frames the meaning gently.

It should not sound like a motivational quote.  
It should sound plain, timeless, and honest.

Strong examples:

- “Let mortality make you present.”
- “Remembering the end clarifies the day.”
- “This day is finite. Let it be lived.”
- “Do not rush past what will not return.”

### Divider

Optional, but useful.

A short, hairline divider can act as a quiet mark of passage between reflection and response.

It should feel structural, not decorative.

### Prompt

Use **one prompt only**.  
One prompt is more coherent than multiple prompts because it feels more like a vow than a journaling exercise.

Strong examples:

- “One thing worth doing with this day:”
- “A small intention for this finite day:”
- “What matters enough to begin now:”
- “What deserves your life today:”

### Primary action

Use a single button beneath the prompt.

Recommended label:

**Begin**

This works best because it feels like crossing a threshold.

Other acceptable options:

- Continue
- Enter

### Footer

Very small, low-contrast microtext at the bottom.

Good examples:

- “Not a warning. An invitation.”
- “A reminder, not a threat.”
- “You are not being hurried. You are being called awake.”

---

## Interaction design

### General rule

Interactions should feel like **acknowledgment**, not stimulation.

### Hover states

- minimal darkening or tonal shift
- no bright color pops
- no flashy animations

### Focus states

- thin outline
- muted accent color
- calm and visible
- never neon or techy

### Transitions

- soft
- slow
- ease-out
- around 150–250ms

### Motion

Optional only.

A very slight fade-in on load is acceptable.  
Avoid motion that feels lively, clever, or app-like.

### Sound

None.

---

## Content and voice

### Tone rules

The writing should be:

- plain
- human
- restrained
- sincere
- timeless

It should not be:

- preachy
- dramatic
- poetic for its own sake
- self-helpy
- productivity-branded

### Voice goal

The page should not say:

- optimize today
- crush the day
- be your best self

It should say:

**this day is finite, so let it be real.**

---

## Coherence rules

To keep the theme conceptually strong:

### Use one of each

- one accent color
- one symbolic watermark
- one main prompt
- one primary action
- one emotional promise

### Emotional promise

**This page helps the user meet the day honestly, without panic.**

### Design filter

Before adding any element, ask:

**Does this deepen presence, or does it merely decorate the page?**

If it only decorates, remove it.

---

## Accessibility and comfort

Minimalism should never come at the expense of comfort.

### Requirements

- strong readable contrast between text and background
- secondary text still comfortably legible
- input and button large enough to feel calm, not fiddly
- focus states clearly visible
- no fragile ultra-light text
- no interactions that rely on color alone

The page should feel restful, but still clear and usable.

## Final design summary

Bone White should feel like a **quiet ritual of perspective**.  
It should bring mortality close enough to sharpen the day, but gently enough to keep the user steady.

It is not about darkness.  
It is not about fear.  
It is not about performance.

It is about this:

**life is brief, and therefore attention is sacred.**
